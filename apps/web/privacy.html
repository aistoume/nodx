Privacy Policy

Effective 2026-06-30 · Updated 2026-07-22 · Applies to all Aicon Solutions products

The short version: Aicon Solutions has no servers that store your data. Our products are local-first. Our products collect no analytics, no telemetry, and no personal information. AI features route your requests directly to the provider you configure (Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google) using your own API key — we never see the content. This website (aicon.solutions) uses Google Analytics to measure aggregate page traffic — see Website analytics.

1 · Who we are

Aicon Solutions ("we", "us") is an independent product studio operated by LaoMos (Aicon Solutions, contact@aicon.solutions). This page applies to every product we ship, including nodx Lens (Chrome extension), nodx desktop (macOS app), and any past or future products listed on aicon.solutions.

2 · What data each product handles

nodx Lens (Chrome extension)

What stays on your device

  • Your AI provider API key (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google), stored in chrome.storage.local
  • Your language, model, and trigger preferences
  • Up to the 100 most recent explanations (text you highlighted + the AI's response)

What leaves your browser

When you click 🔍 to explain a highlighted phrase, an HTTPS request goes directly from your browser to the AI provider you configured. The request contains only:

  • The text you highlighted
  • A short prompt asking the AI to explain it
  • Your API key (as an authorization header)

The request goes to api.anthropic.com, api.openai.com, or generativelanguage.googleapis.com depending on which provider you chose. nodx Lens has no server of its own that sees this traffic.

What we don't do

  • No analytics, no telemetry, no crash reports
  • No account registration or sign-in
  • No selling, transferring, or aggregating user data
  • No use of user data for advertising or credit decisions

nodx desktop (macOS app)

What stays on your device

  • All your topics, messages, decisions, and reasoning traces (local SQLite database in your app data folder)
  • Your AI provider API key (stored in macOS Keychain, same encryption Safari uses)
  • Your preferences (theme, hotkey settings, layout)

What leaves your device

Same model as nodx Lens: an in-process gateway inside the app makes HTTPS requests directly to your chosen AI provider using your own API key. Nothing is sent to any nodx-owned server. If you opt into Claude Code subscription mode, the app spawns the claude CLI on your machine, which in turn calls Anthropic under your Claude Code subscription — nodx never touches the OAuth token.

System permissions we request

  • Accessibility (⌥+E system-wide selection capture) — required for the app to synthesise ⌘+C so it can read what you highlighted in another app. The synthesised copy is reverted within ~120 ms so your clipboard content is preserved.

nodx Lens for Android

Applies to v1.0.0 and later (sideload APK and Google Play).

What stays on your device

  • Your AI provider API keys (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google / OpenRouter) — stored in app-private SharedPreferences, never transmitted anywhere except directly to that provider
  • Saved screenshots and generated images (your device's photo library, Pictures/nodx)
  • The activity log — prompts, AI answers, search queries and links, with thumbnails — stored in app-private storage, capped at 200 entries, delete any entry with a long-press
  • Your action-wheel customisation

What leaves your device

Exactly one kind of traffic: when you tap the bubble and pick an action, the cropped screenshot region and your prompt are sent over HTTPS directly to the AI provider you selected, authenticated with your own key. There is no nodx server, no analytics, no crash reporting, no ads SDK.

System permissions we request

  • Display over other apps — draws the floating bubble, the region selector, and the result card.
  • Screen capture (MediaProjection) — captures one frame when you tap the bubble. Android asks for your consent per session.
  • Accessibility service (optional) — the "long-lived capture" toggle. If you enable the nodx screenshot service, the app uses the accessibility takeScreenshot API so you are not re-asked for screen-share consent after every lock/unlock. The service declares no event types, reads no window content, and captures only at the moment you tap the bubble. It is used for no other purpose. You can leave it off and use per-session consent instead.
  • Notifications — the persistent notification Android requires for the bubble's foreground service, which also hosts the Stop button.

2b · Website analytics (aicon.solutions only)

This website uses Google Analytics 4 (gtag.js) to understand aggregate traffic: pages viewed, referral source, device type, and approximate location derived from IP. Google processes this data on our behalf and sets cookies in your browser; see Google's privacy policy. We use it only to measure which pages and campaigns bring visitors — no ad personalization, and we never feed it any content from our products.

This applies to the website only. The products themselves (nodx Lens, nodx desktop, nodx Lens for Android) contain no analytics of any kind, as described above. To opt out of Google Analytics across the web, use the official browser add-on.

3 · Data we handle (Chrome Web Store terminology)

Per Chrome Web Store's data-handling categories:

  • Authentication information — your AI provider API key, stored locally in your browser (Lens) or the macOS Keychain (desktop). Never leaves your device except in HTTPS requests you initiate to the provider you chose.
  • Website content — the text you explicitly highlight and choose to explain. Sent only to the AI provider you configured. Not retained by us.

We do not handle:

  • Personally identifiable information (name, email, phone, address)
  • Health information
  • Financial information
  • Personal communications
  • Location data
  • Web browsing history
  • User activity beyond what you deliberately trigger
